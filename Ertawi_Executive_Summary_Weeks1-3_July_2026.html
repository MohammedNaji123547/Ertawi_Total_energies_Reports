Bottom Line

EV correction: the three weekly reports did not capture 102,546 USD of earned value from additional scope performed during the period — 33,895 USD on Well Pads & Questor and 68,652 USD on Flowlines/GRE Lines & AG-25. Once added, the picture changes substantially: corrected EV rises from 111,650 to 214,196 USD, and overall CPI improves from 0.41 to 0.78. Flowlines/GRE Lines & AG-25 swings to a favourable CPI of 1.14 (from 0.30); Well Pads & Questor improves to 0.63 but remains below 1.0.

Data gap: the source data for this additional scope shows a fourth line item that was cut off / not fully legible in the sheet provided. The 102,546 USD figure above reflects only the three confirmed rows — the corrected totals could move further once that item is confirmed. It's also not yet possible to allocate this additional scope down to the four individual projects (Well Pads & Valve Station, Flow Line & Cable Work, Questor, AG-25); it is shown as a separate unallocated line until that split is available.

Underlying cost picture is unchanged by this correction: IGCC staff cost remains a real driver of actual cost — running well above its earned value across all three weeks — and materials cost on Well Pads & Questor more than doubled week over week (0 → 19,000 → 38,300). These are cost-side issues, independent of the EV correction, and still warrant review.

Recommendation: (1) confirm the missing fourth line item and the project-level split for the additional scope EV; (2) re-baseline future weekly reports to capture this scope going forward so it isn't missed again; (3) continue to track IGCC staffing cost and Well Pads & Questor materials spend, which remain the primary cost-side concerns even after the EV correction.
